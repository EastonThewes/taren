# **THE CONSTITUTION OF VAELDRIN**

_Ratified by the Council of Nine and upheld by the Office of the High Speaker_

---
## **Preamble**

In order to preserve the peace, prosperity, and governance of Vaeldrin, unify the dominions of Rivermere and the surrounding provinces, and ensure the stability of law and order, the citizens and ruling bodies establish this Constitution of Vaeldrin. It defines the authority, duties, election procedures, and checks governing the Council of Nine and the High Speaker.

---
# **ARTICLE I — The Council of Nine**

## **Section 1. Composition**

1. The Legislature of Vaeldrin shall be vested in a council composed of **nine seated councilors**, collectively known as the **Council of Nine**.
2. Councilors hold office for **nine-year terms** with no restriction on the number of renewed terms.
## **Section 2. Eligibility**
1. Any citizen of Vaeldrin may serve as a councilor.
2. In practice, candidates typically include wealthy patrons, nobles, guild masters, distinguished merchants, and retired commanders.
## **Section 3. Vacancies**
A seat becomes vacant upon:
- voluntary resignation,
- removal by the Council,
- death,
- or a councilor’s resignation to seek the High Speakership.

Appointments to unfinished terms shall serve the remainder of the original term

---
# **ARTICLE II — Appointment of Councilors**
## **Section 1. Nomination**
1. Any sitting councilor may nominate **one candidate** to fill a vacant seat.
2. Nominations require a **second** by another councilor.
## **Section 2. Election**
1. A candidate must receive a **simple majority of the sitting councilors**.
2. Abstentions do not count toward the majority.
3. In the event of a tie:
    - The High Speaker shall cast the deciding vote.
    - If the High Speaker’s office is vacant, voting shall repeat until a simple majority is reached.
---
# **ARTICLE III — Removal of Councilors**
## **Section 1. Grounds for Removal**
A councilor may be removed due to:
- misconduct or corruption,
- dereliction of duty,
- acts causing harm to Vaeldrin or Rivermere,
- or failure to maintain trust or influence.
## **Section 2. Vote**
1. Removal requires a **simple majority** of sitting councilors.
2. In the event of a tie, the **High Speaker** casts the deciding vote.
3. A removed councilor may not serve again for **five years**.
---
# **ARTICLE IV — Reelection Procedures**
1. Each year, the council conducts a retention vote for the seat whose term expires.
2. A councilor must earn a **simple majority** to maintain the seat.
3. Ties are resolved by the High Speaker.
---
# **ARTICLE V — Election of the High Speaker**
## **Section 1. Office**
The High Speaker serves as the head of state and presiding authority over the Council, holding a **ten-year term** with no term limit.
## **Section 2. Eligibility**
Any citizen of Vaeldrin may be nominated.
## **Section 3. Nomination**
1. Each councilor may nominate **one candidate**.
2. Nominations must be seconded.
## **Section 4. Election**
1. A nominee must secure a **two-thirds majority** (six of nine councilors).
2. If no nominee achieves this majority:
    - After **three rounds**, the lowest-voted nominee is removed.
    - Voting continues until a candidate receives two-thirds.
## **Section 5. Deadlock**
If no High Speaker is elected within **thirty days**:
- The **longest-serving councilor** becomes **Acting Speaker**,
- The election must continue until a High Speaker is chosen.
## **Section 6. Resignation of Candidates**
Any councilor seeking Speakership shall resign their seat before candidacy.  
The vacated seat **must** be filled before Speaker elections proceed.

---
# **ARTICLE VI — Removal of the High Speaker**
1. The Council may conduct a **vote of no confidence**.
2. Removal requires a **two-thirds majority** of sitting councilors.
3. Upon removal, a new High Speaker shall be elected to complete the remainder of the term.
---
# **ARTICLE VII — Legislative Authority**
## **Section 1. Council-Initiated Legislation**
1. Any councilor may propose legislation.
2. Passage requires a **simple majority**.
3. The High Speaker may refuse enactment.
4. The Council may override the refusal via another simple majority vote.
## **Section 2. Speaker-Initiated Directives**
1. The High Speaker may issue executive directives.
2. The Council may veto such directives via simple majority.
3. If the vote is tied, the directive stands.
---
# **ARTICLE VIII — Emergency Powers**
In times of war, crisis, or economic collapse, the **First Merchant** may be granted temporary emergency powers.  
These powers cease immediately once the crisis subsides and normal governance resumes.

---
# **ARTICLE IX — Voting Standards**

1. Abstentions are considered non-votes.
2. Secret ballots may be used at the Council’s discretion.
3. Political influence, alliances, bribery, and blackmail—though unofficial—are acknowledged as factors shaping votes.
---
# **ARTICLE X — Amendments**
This Constitution may be amended by:
- a unanimous vote of the [[Council of Nine]],
- ratification by the [[High Speaker]].
